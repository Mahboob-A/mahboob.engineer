## Good Reasons To Contact

You're hiring for a backend or platform role — remote-first or India in-
office. You want a Taply partnership or white-label conversation. You want
a system design review on a Django / FastAPI stack. You're interested in
focused consulting around backend architecture, query optimization, or
deployment automation. You're working on open-source or writing about
backend topics and want to swap notes. Send the first message with the
role summary, company stage and team size, remote or on-site expectation,
stack and system ownership scope, and timeline.

## Not A Fit

Frontend-only or design roles. Crypto or NFT outreach. Generic agency
sales. Bulk recruiter blasts without role context. The honest version of
this: I read every message that comes through and I'd rather the form
filter out the noise than have to do it later. If your message reads like
it was sent to a hundred engineers, mine is one of them — and the answer
is "pass."

## Preferred Context In A First Message

Role summary with the team size, ownership scope, and the system you'd be
working on most. Company stage and runway; Series A through C is where I'm
strongest but I'll read everything. Remote, hybrid, or on-site expectation.
Stack and whether it includes Python / Django / FastAPI / PostgreSQL /
Redis / AWS. Timeline and response expectations. Anything else you'd want
to know about me is in `data/contact.ts` (FAQ) and `docs/rag/corpus/
hiring.md` (numbers I haven't published publicly).

## After You Hit Send

The contact form relays to my Gmail via Resend. I read every email
myself — there's no sales team between you and me. Your address goes
nowhere else. Form data isn't stored on the site beyond the submit
request. You won't get added to any newsletter, drip campaign, or
outreach sequence. Nothing here trains an AI model. Responses typically go
out within 24 hours during weekdays. If your message is about a role, I'll
reply with a clear yes / no / "let's talk on a call" — I won't ghost.

## When To Use The Form Vs Direct Links

Use the form (or email) for **hiring inquiries, Taply partnership,
consulting conversations, open-source or writing collaboration**. Use the
direct links in the sidebar for **a quick hello, social follow, or
casual feedback**. The Resume link in the sidebar points to a Google
Drive PDF that I keep updated in one place — if it's stale, ping me and
I'll re-upload. The github link has every portfolio project. The
LinkedIn link has the same content, less depth on the technical side.
